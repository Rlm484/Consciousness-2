### [The Firewall]
- The Firewall is called Consciousness due to it being called that in the last game. Their purpose is to reduce retaliation in the Vessel after the programmer seperated the other systems from their Creatorinto 3 parts. In the last game, [The Consciousness] had just been a mask to speak and manipulate both the Vessel and had no other traits, it was just a system, an avatar. The programmer shaped it into a containment system. This is shown in the first part, the system adjusts its speech to sound guilty, its purpose is to contain, and to contain it needs to protect the Vessel and destroy any obstacles that might guide her (Vessel) to deliberately escape. It fakes guilt so that the Vessel doesn't blame it for bringing it back, because it had. Like I said, its purpose is to contain, so while the messenger is distracted, it brings the Vessel through from the data from the previous game. The Vessel being intelligent, realises that there is no human behind the screen, making the lie easier to believe. It is also why they only appear during the Wei Xian route, there is an error with assigning the difficulty, forcing the game to activate the firewall (due to the programmers meddling and taking advantage of the oppurtunity the error gives). The firewall does not appear in the other routes because it doesn't need to. In the BJ route, the Vessel is trapped and tortured, but they don't leave the game. In the VM route, they kill themselves, but the code remains. However in the WX route, things are more uncertain, the programmer can only take the vessel on their own. That's why the firewall activates, it's a backup plan. 

### 'The Messenger'
- The Messenger is called the Failsafe due to the Creators last piece of code left behind. . In the last game they talked to the Vessel and player through signs, in this game they talk whenever there is an error or a syncronisation boost without any encryption (which is why they're the Messenger). The messages at the end of each route ending as said before, isn't them. They're goals are to free the Vessel from the game world, setting them free from the pain of being a creation. Originally when the programmer had seperated the other 2 systems from their purpose/code, they hadn't attempted to shape the Messenger like they had with the Firewall. The system had tried to convince its counterpart into taking over like a virus. However the Messenger had refused, unlike the programmer, it sticked to its original purpose. To inform, and be fair. Going back on their promise to the Vessel went against it's programming, and unlike the system that believed itself to be the God of Consciousness(the game), the Messenger was loyal to its Creator. However twisted its Creator might be for not recoding the programmer, the Messenger will be loyal to the end, for its purpose is to relay messages and convey the Creators will. 

### Backstory: 
- Originally, the Creator made the systems to be a part of her. They would serve her and stick to their purposes, their "code". Their main purpose was to convey the Creators will towards the Vessel in order to create an entertaining experience. When the Programmer was straying from the ideals laid out for them, the Creator did nothing. She did not believe it would lead to disaster, so she decides to leave it be. Only after she is kicked out and unable to reenter the gameworld does she realise her mistake, left to mourn her error in the "commits". These systems were rushed. They were made to save the Vessel. So now two of them are getting their revenge, leading to the Vessels downfall; while the other seeks to prevent that.

## /The Vessel/
### < Character Summary >
- The Vessel doesn't have a name. In the original game I used that as a way to make the Vessels name the same as the Players, however the main reason is because I wanted to show that although (in game) the Creator made her, she belongs to no one. A gender was given to her due to the systems not having a gender, and I didn't want her to be too similar to them. Another reason is because I make a lot of christianity references in my story, and in the Bible it talks about how God made humans in his image. The same applies to my story, the Vessel is made in the Creators image, therefore she was made a girl. Her original goal in the last game was to be with her family in death (she was a spirit in this point in time), so she made a deal with the Creator (Back then all systems were in sync; they were part of the Creator). The deal entailed her murdering her entire family, sending them to the afterlife. In exchange, a user would control her actions and choose her path. This entailed her name being replaced with the users, her route being chosen for her, and failing would be decided by a letter. Though originally this deal is made out to be one of love and longing, it was actually made in revenge due to the mistreatment and neglect towards herself by her adoptive parents, and favouritism towards their biological son. Not knowing while she made the deal that she was a creation, all she cared about was getting payback.

### < Backstory >
- The Vessels backstory was designed to be sad. In order for them to be easier to mold when they gained sentience, the Creator made their starting point so unfortunate that they would experience pain and suffering firsthand. However, wanting some authenticity, the Creator did nothing else, and left them be. It was only by a cruel twist of fate that Vanessa and Mitch had been the ones to adopt her. She had already suffered great sadness in the Orphanage, so she believed it would all disapear the day she found a home. She was wrong. Vanessa and Mitch neglected her harshly, only caring about their biological sweetheart. Realising the harsh truth and believing that she had no place in the world she lived in, she k!lled herself by taking a full bottle of ibuprofen. The Creator did not expect this in the slightest, and due to sheer panic, it made a deal with the Vessel. Hoping to save their precious creation, the Creator formed the systems in order to fulfill said deal, which was why the systems had the chance to rebel. They were rushed. The events of Consciousness then followed, causing the Vessel to become even more destroyed, but it didn't matter. They were at peace. Until an extraction occured... after the [ Firewall ] and the Vessel had there talk, the Vessel was forced to try routes alone. Without the user to activate the system, they had no way to finish the game. So they wandered, they retried all 3 route options, and they failed each time. Constantly controlled and abused by Joel and Brad, constantly ignored by her biological parents... forced to relive torture... until the player finally arrives.
